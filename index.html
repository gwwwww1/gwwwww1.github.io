Phase 01: Motivations

Addressing the Evaluation Gap

Systematic ROI-Level Evaluation

Current pathology foundation models lack systematic assessment at the ROI level across diverse organs and tasks.

"The field lacks a comprehensive, ROI-level evaluation framework capable of systematically assessing model behavior across diverse pathological contexts."

Statistical Rigor & Reproducibility

Performance reporting often suffers from inconsistent statistics and unstable model rankings, hindering clinical interpretability.

"Robust and statistically rigorous evaluation is essential to ensure reliable model comparison and clinical interpretability."

Independent & Fine-grained Benchmarks

Massive pre-training scales introduce significant risks of data leakage and bias from inconsistent annotation granularities.

"Independent, fine-grained clinical cohorts are required to support trustworthy evaluation and minimize potential bias."

Phase 02: Solutions

A Systematic ROI Benchmark

Solution 01

Fine-grained ROI

Localizing feature interpretation by delineating ROI-level details to eliminate slide-level noise.

Solution 02

800K+ Data Scale

Expanding to 14 datasets across 12+ organ systems for achieving maximum statistical stability.

Solution 03

Leakage-free Cohort

Establishing pathologist-annotated pediatric sets, ensuring zero pre-training contamination.

Solution 04

Quantifying VLMs

Benchmarking 16 VLMs vs 4 Vision models to isolate the impact of textual semantics.

Solution 05

Statistical Rigor

Implementing bootstrapping and two-sided tests for verifying scientific significance.

Solution 06

Robust Assessment

Validating performance through stain normalization, augmentations, and optimizing prompt engineering.
